# G&S Good Stuff — 5-Year Business Plan (2026–2030)

## Mission
Scale a premium dahlia tuber operation from 100 tubers to 80,000+ tubers over five years, building a recognized regional brand with direct-to-consumer and wholesale channels.

## Location Profile
- **Address:** Addison, NY 14801
- **Acreage:** 16 acres (expandable to adjacent parcels)
- **USDA Zone:** 5b
- **Coordinates:** 42.10°N, 77.23°W
- **Elevation:** 1,020 ft
- **Valley:** Canisteo River valley
- **Last frost:** May 15
- **First frost:** October 1
- **Growing season:** 138 days

## Revenue Model
- **Retail (DTC):** $8–12/tuber via gsgoodstuff.com storefront
- **Wholesale:** $4–6/tuber to garden centers, farm markets
- **Premium varieties:** $15–25/tuber for rare/new introductions
- **Workshops/tours:** Future revenue stream (Year 3+)

**Theme:** Quadruple production, establish brand

**Theme:** Hit profitability, automate operations

- Full 16-acre utilization

- Target 80 varieties, 10,000 produced, $54K revenue

## Year 4 — 2029: Regional Brand
**Theme:** Become the go-to dahlia source in the Northeast

- Wholesale partnerships with 20+ garden centers
- Farmers market presence (3 markets)
- Employee team (2 FT, 4 seasonal)
- Target 120 varieties, 32,000 produced, $160K revenue

## Year 5 — 2030: Full Scale
**Theme:** 80K tubers, national reach

- Online sales shipping nationwide
- Catalog of 150+ varieties
- Breeding program for proprietary varieties
- Target 150 varieties, 82,432 produced, $480K revenue

## Key Assumptions
- Average division yield: 4 tubers per clump
- Sell-through rate improves from 50% (Y1) to 73% (Y5)
- Average retail price holds at $9/tuber
- Wholesale at 50% of retail
- Attrition/loss rate: 10% of planted stock
- Growing season constrains planting window to late May–early June

## Risk Factors
- Late/early frost destroying planted stock
- Dahlia virus (DMV) contamination
- Market saturation in dahlia tubers
- Supply chain issues for rare varieties
- Storage failures (cooler temp excursion)
